Varylab - Discrete Surface Optimization

Varylab is a software developed at Berlin Institute of Technology by members of the geometry group. It is supported by the DFG Research Center MATHEON, and the SFB/TR 109 Discretization in Geometry and Dynamics. Varylab implements various variational principles for discrete surfaces, e.g., planarity of faces, equal lengths of edges, curvature of parameter curves. It is designed to be an extensible and modular tool for experiments with discrete surfaces in pure mathematics and applications in industrial geometry.


Varylab Team Members:
  • Thilo Roerig
  • Stefan Sechelmann


Features:
  • Various energy plug-ins
  • Conformal and curvature line parameterization
  • Remeshing of discrete surfaces
  • Comprehensive graphical user interface
  • Data visualization modules
  • Python scripting API
